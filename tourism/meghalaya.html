Tourism in Meghalaya is a captivating experience, offering a blend of stunning natural landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and unique traditions. Known as the "Abode of Clouds," the state is famous for its lush greenery, breathtaking waterfalls, and mist-covered hills, making it a paradise for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts. Meghalaya is home to some of India’s most remarkable natural wonders, such as the living root bridges in Cherrapunji, the crystal-clear waters of Dawki, and the majestic Nohkalikai Falls, which is the tallest plunge waterfall in India. The state also boasts charming hill stations like Shillong, which is often called the "Scotland of the East," and tranquil villages like Mawlynnong, Asia’s cleanest village. Rich in culture, Meghalaya offers travelers a glimpse into the traditional lifestyles of its indigenous Khasi, Garo, and Jaintia tribes. Whether it’s exploring the cave systems, trekking through verdant hills, or experiencing local festivals and cuisine, Meghalaya provides an unparalleled experience for those looking to explore the natural beauty and cultural depth of Northeast India.
